About Me

Hello! I am Abby Joseph, a Roboticist and a creative. I am originally from Southern Maryland, which is where I first discovered my love for STEM, and particularly technology. For my undergraduate studies, I went to the University of Maryland, Baltimore County where I was a Meyerhoff Scholar and student-athlete studying Computer Engineering.

While at UMBC, I discovered my love for digital design, robotics, and research - since I love contributing to novel technologies. In working in technical environments alongside my experiences as an athlete, the concept of teamwork and working towards a goal greater than any individual was further reinforced for me. I love teamwork and collaboration because I believe you will achieve the most well-rounded solution when many people with different perspectives can contribute. Personally, I like to introduce creative and innovative ideas to technical projects, which is something that I have been able to do in both my research in the AIRO Lab with Dr. Joseph Moore and my personal technical projects.

At Johns Hopkins University, through the GEM fellowship sponsored by the University and Draper Laboratory, I had the opportunity to further develop my technical skillset and knowledge base so that I may better contribute to the field of Robotics. I hope that, going forward, I can continue to help drive advancements within Robotics and embodied AI that are human-centered and help with space exploration and making scientific discoveries.

In my free time, I am often trying to blend my creative pursuits with my love for Robotics. I want to help show the ways in which these identities can coexist and that technological advancements don't always need to be feared.
